BWF Primer — reference

Personal quick reference. Program by Nick-E. Recommended on the r/bodyweightfitness beginner wiki.

Summary

The BWF Primer is a free bodyweight routine for beginners: a 14-day build-up (low intensity, technique focus), then a full routine of six exercises, 3× per week.

The six exercises (full body):

  • Push-up (upper)
  • Row or reverse push-up (upper)
  • Squat (lower)
  • Glute bridge (lower / posterior)
  • Deadbug (core)
  • Bird dog (core)

You can run build-up days on consecutive days, or every other day (~28 days) if you need more recovery or time.

Full BWF Primer (after build-up)

3× per week. For static holds, treat 1 rep ≈ 2 seconds (e.g. 3×10 = 3×20s). Progression lists align with the BWSF routine.

Exercise Progression Sets × reps Rest
Deadbugs (or plank) Core A 3×6–10 60s
Bird dog Core B 3×6–10 60s
Push-ups Push-up 3×5–12 2 min
Rows Rows 3×5–12 2 min
Squats Squat 3×8–15 90s
Bridges / Hinges Posterior chain 3×8–15 90s

Start at the low end of rep ranges; add one rep per session until you hit the top, then move to a harder progression and reset to the bottom of the range.

Graduation (optional)

Move on to the BWSF routine when you can do:

  • 3×8 push-ups with ~1s hold at the bottom of each rep
  • 3×8 horizontal rows with ~1s hold at the bottom of each rep

Or maintain: continue this program 2× per week if you are happy at this level.
